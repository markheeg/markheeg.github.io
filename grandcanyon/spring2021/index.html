Training

Training for the Grand Canyon is important as being in good shape allows you to safely hike the canyon for a much more enjoyable experience.

Get out and start walking as much as you can. The trip will be here before you know it.
Over the last month or two before the trip, increase your exercise.
If you can, try and incorporate hills or stadium bleachers into your routine.

Also, if possible wear your pack every couple of weeks so you get used to carrying the weight and adjusting it.

How to Dress

It is recommended that you dress in layers so that you can adjust your comfort level as you hike by removing or adding clothing as you need. Also, it is about 20° cooler at the top of the Canyon than it is at the bottom, so you will be heating up as you descend into the Canyon.

I recommend wearing the following when you start the descent into the Canyon
  • Layers
    • Base Layer - Long Sleeve Shirt, Wool Recommended
    • Mid Layer - Light Fleece or Thick Wool
    • Protective Layer - Lightweight Insulated Jacket
  • Hiking Pants with zip off legs
  • Optional Items (based on weather)
    • Knit Cap
    • Gloves
    • Long Underwear
    • Rain Jacket
